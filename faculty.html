Call for Faculty Proposals – UR2PhD at UTA (Fall 2025 Only)

We invite UTA faculty to submit proposals to lead a UR2PhD research cohort during Fall 2025.

What is a Cohort?

A cohort consists of:

  • 1 faculty mentor
  • 1 graduate student mentor
  • 2 undergraduate researchers

The cohort engages in a yearlong mentored research experience supported by a national UR2PhD curriculum designed to build research identity and prepare students for graduate school.

Proposal Requirements

  • Project title and description, scoped to 1 semester
  • Identified graduate mentor (must be able to enroll in the grad course); graduate mentors who have completed the course are eligible for the continuing mentor stipend.
  • Two undergraduate researchers identified (must be able to enroll in SAME undergrad course) or Your criteria for your undergraduate researchers (e.g., GPA, courses, year)

Proposal deadline: Monday, August 19, 2025

We will fund up to 10 cohorts ($1000 stipend for grad mentor; $500 scholarship for undergrads. If more proposals are submitted, a committee will review and select the final cohort list. Additional undergraduate students may join your project but must be supported with $500 per student from non-grant funds (e.g., startup, IDC).

If undergraduate researchers are not identified at the time of submission, we will issue a call to CSE undergrads, gather application materials, and allow you to select students for your cohort.

Graduate Mentor Expectations & Compensation

Graduate mentors will enroll in a national virtual course (choose one time):

  • Tuesdays, 2–3PM CT (3–4PM ET)
  • Wednesdays, 11AM–12PM CT (12–1PM ET)

Course dates: September 9 – December 10, 2025

Graduate mentors who complete the course will receive a $1,000 stipend paid directly by CRA. To be eligible, they must:

  • Attend and actively engage in the course
  • Complete all assigned coursework
  • Have no more than two unexcused absences
  • Submit all required CRA forms (W-9/W-8BEN, Independent Contractor Agreement, Direct Deposit form)

International students should check with the UTA Office of International Education (OIE) to confirm stipend eligibility. They may still participate even if they cannot be compensated.

Undergraduate Participation

Each cohort includes two undergraduate researchers. They will also enroll in a national virtual course (choose one time):

  • Tuesdays, 7–9PM CT (8–10PM ET)
  • Wednesdays, 6–8PM CT (7–9PM ET)

Course dates: September 23 – December 7, 2025

Undergrads who complete the course will receive a $500 scholarship disbursed via myMav the following semester.

They must:

  • Attend and engage in course sessions
  • Complete all coursework
  • Have no more than two unexcused absences

Additional Benefits

  • Travel funding: Up to $2,000 for presenting research outcomes
  • REU continuation funding: Up to $8,000 for undergrads who complete the course and submit a proposal
  • Continued mentorship stipend: Graduate mentors may receive $250/year for ongoing mentorship
